About Me

Web Developer. Mechanical Engineer. Entrepreneur. Three very different fields, yet they align more closely than you may think. I currently work as a Designs Engineer at the Richmond, CA Chevron refinery creating new, innovative systems to ensure constant operational availability and continuous improvement in our fuel-delivery systems.

During my free time over the past year, I have been teaching myself to code and learning the building blocks of web development. Between courses on Udemy, YouTube tutorials, and the vast array of resources available on the internet, learning to code is a continually accessible and enjoyable, yet challenging, endeavor.

As the Internet of Things develops, we will continue to see the lines blur between the physical and digital fields; I believe that an understanding of both fields will be key to any current and future entrepreneurial pursuits.

My biggest project so far is CampZone, a comprehensive, full-stack application that allows users to create profiles, follow each other, and, most importantly, create landing pages for their favorite campgrounds with the ability to upload their own photos, Google maps integration, and the option to leave comments and reviews. Some of my other projects, such as a Patatap.com clone and an RGB Guessing Game are also displayed below.

If you’re interested in getting in touch with me, feel free to contact me via the “How To Reach Me” form, or send me a message on LinkedIn / Github. I am available for part-time freelancing for either front-end or full-stack development work.
